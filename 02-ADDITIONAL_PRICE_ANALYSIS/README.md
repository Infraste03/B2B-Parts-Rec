# 02 - Advanced Analysis: Price Dynamics and Customer Behavior

## Overview and Motivation

This directory contains a suite of advanced analytical scripts designed to investigate a core research question: **To what extent do price dynamics influence purchasing behavior in a B2B context, compared to the well-documented effects in the B2C sector?**

Building upon the initial exploratory data analysis, this investigation moves beyond static descriptions to uncover and quantify the dynamic interplay between price, time, and purchasing decisions. The primary goal is to determine if concepts traditionally associated with B2C markets—such as price sensitivity and seasonal demand—are significant drivers in the industrial spare parts domain.

## Key Quantitative Findings

The core findings of this analysis are summarized in the table below, generated by the `analyze_advanced_metrics.py` script. The results consistently indicate that purchasing behavior in this B2B context is driven more by operational need and seasonality than by price incentives.

| Metric | Value | Interpretation | Conclusion for B2B |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Price Elasticity of Demand (β₁) **| -0.069 | Highly inelastic demand (close to 0). | Price changes have a minimal impact on sales volume. |
| **Granger Causality (p-value)** | 0.768 | Not statistically significant (p > 0.05). | Past price changes do not help predict future sales. |
| **Mutual Information (Sales; Month)** | 0.503 bits| Seasonality provides significant information. | Seasonality is a strong predictive signal. |
| **Mutual Information (Sales; Discount)**| 0.008 bits| Discounts provide very little information. | Price incentives are a weak predictive signal. |
| **Partial Correlation (Price-Volume)**| r = NaN | The price-volume link is too weak to compute reliably after removing seasonality. | The price-volume relationship is negligible. |

In summary, the analysis strongly suggests that, for this industrial spare parts dataset, demand is **inelastic**. Customers purchase items based on need (e.g., maintenance cycles, project requirements), and are largely insensitive to price fluctuations or discounts. The most significant predictive factor for sales volume is seasonality.

## Methodology & Scripts

The analysis is performed by a series of modular Python scripts. The results (plots and data tables) are saved in the `/results/` subdirectory.

-   **`analyze_advanced_metrics.py`**: The core quantitative script. It calculates the advanced econometric metrics summarized in the table above.
-   **`analyze_price_dynamics.py`**: Provides a macro-level view of market dynamics, tracking price trends, inferred discounts, and sales seasonality.
-   **`analyze_item_behavior.py`**: Focuses on item-level characteristics, using a quadrant analysis to segment products into behavioral archetypes (e.g., "Seasonal Staples," "Price-Driven").
-   **`analyze_advanced_behavior.py`**: Investigates purchasing cycles and price sensitivity at the product-category level.
-   **`verify_repurchases.py`**: A lightweight utility script for quick, console-based validation of repurchase statistics.
